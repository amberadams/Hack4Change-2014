Making History



Open Data Executive Order

On May 12th Mayor Karl Dean will sign into effect his Open Data Executive Order that requires all departments of the Metropolitan government to open their data through the Nashville open data API. This portal will be launched on May 12th and will be pre-populated with 15 robust data sets.

A step towards Transparency

This is a historical step for the greater metropolitan area of Nashville, and one that will further its goals of transparency and communication with local citizens. This data will provide insight into the services and benefits provided by the Metropolitan government and enable citizens to interact more directly with those departments as well as create technology which will enhance our community's quality of life.

Open Data = Real World Applications

The data opened by this executive order will be used by Nashvilleâ€™s technology community to create real world applications that can have a dramatic impact on the lives of Nashville citizens and those who seek to visit and work here. Local software developers demonstrated how this model works during last year's National Day of Civic Hacking with the success of the wESL application, http://www.eslmap.com. Now with direct lines to deeper content, the Nashville community has the opportunity to do so much more.
